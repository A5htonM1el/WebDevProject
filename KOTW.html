This Week's Case

The Bind, Torture, Kill (BTK) Killer

Background

Between 1974 and 1991 Dennis Rader murdered 10 people including two children mostly by binding torturing and killing which is where he got the infamous name from. Outside his horrific acts he was a family man. Having a wife and two kids. His job was installing security systems for people, held many different degrees, was also a president of his local Lutheran church council. He held many disturbing fantasies such as domination, torturing and murdering women unfortunately these turned into reality when he was let go from a job called Cessna in 1973

Crimes

Rader committed his first murders on January 15th, 1974, by strangling four family members including two children. At this point none of his victims had been sexually assaulted but he did take some souvenirs. His next victim was a 21-year-old woman who he stabbed in April 1974. He also encountered her brother who managed to escape despite being shot. Over the next two decades Rader killed five more women. His next victim whom he strangled in March of 1977 and then followed the death of his next victim in December 1977. Unfortunately, Rader waited 8 years before killing his next victim who was his neighbour. He killed her in her home in 1985, and he reportedly later took her body to his church where he took pictures of her in bondage. The last two murders were committed in 1991 which was a 28-year-old woman of two and in 1991 when he strangled a 62-year-old woman in her home. Cases went cold

Investigation

Even though the BTK killer communicated with the public by writing letters such as late in the year of 1974 he detailed January murders and said that “the code words for me will be bind them, torture them, kill them, B.T.K” which then he left in the Wichita Public Library the police were not able to gain any concrete evidence on Dennis which is why the cases subsequently went cold. In 2004, which was Rader's 30th anniversary of his first murders the local paper ran a feature in which they speculated that the btk killer must have died or been imprisoned. This enraged Rader. He responded by sending evidence to the police showing that he is alive and not imprisoned or leaving evidence from previous murderers around Wichita. Stupidly he asked the police in 2005 if they would be able to trace a floppy disk which he wished to send them. While police lied and said no when they received the floppy disk, they were able to trace it back to Dennis Rader's church where Rader's DNA was then matched to the seamen found at the crime scene.

Outcome

After finding out who the BTK killer was he was arrested in February of 2005 after confessing to the crimes he expressed how shocked he was that the police had lied to him. Soon later in June the BTK killer also known as Dennis Rader whom everyone liked and trusted pled guilty and two months later he was sentenced to 10 consecutive life terms.
